How Ochilar works

Break reminders nag, block, or force a pause — so they only work if you're disciplined. Ochilar doesn't ask for any: it runs quietly alongside your work and does its bit while you keep going.

Edge line
A thin line along the top edge. Instead of being an annoying timer, it fills green → amber → red as you actually work — and quietly drains back when you step away and rest. It tracks real fatigue, not the clock.
Blink reminders
Every few minutes, a few soft screen pulses. Staring at a screen cuts your blink rate by more than half — the main cause of dry, tired eyes — so this gently prompts a blink.
Screen warmth
As your session adds up, the whole screen slowly warms and dims a touch, then eases back when you rest.
Drift · optional, off by default
After a long focused stretch, a soft shape drifts in from the edge to pull your gaze for a moment. A gentle attention switch.

What actually helps

Three things ease tired eyes: blinking more, less glare, and looking far away now and then. Ochilar helps with the first two, automatically. The far-away look is the one part no app can do for you — so glance out a window once in a while too.

Privacy

No accounts, no telemetry — nothing ever leaves your computer. To tell whether you're working or resting, Ochilar just checks whether the mouse is moving and how long since your last key or click. It never sees what you type.

One thing to be clear on

Ochilar does not prevent eye damage and does not improve your vision.

It's a comfort aid for tired, dry eyes — not a medical device or a treatment. Persistent eye pain, headaches, or changes in your eyesight are for an optometrist, not an app.
